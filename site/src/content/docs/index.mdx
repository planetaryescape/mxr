## offline

<div class="spread">
  <div class="narrative">
    <h3>No signal. <em>No problem.</em></h3>
    <p>Open mxr on a plane with the wifi off. Triage. Reply. Draft. Snooze. Opening a message is a SQLite read, not a fetch, so there is no network round trip waiting on you. When you land and your laptop reconnects, mxr finishes everything you queued.</p>
    <p>Your archive is on disk, not in someone else's cloud. Years of email, every attachment, every label: local, indexed, yours.</p>

List the apology email you sent in March. mxr indexes your mail with <a href="https://github.com/quickwit-oss/tantivy">Tantivy</a>, the BM25 engine behind Quickwit, with field boosts on subject, body, sender, and attachment names. ```bash
mxr storage --by sender --limit 20
mxr stale --mine --older-than-days 14
mxr wrapped --year 2025
mxr search "subject:invoice" --format json | jq -r '.[].from' | sort -u

## email your agent can <em>operate</em>

<p>Anything you can do with email, your agent can do. Not "compose a draft and stop." Actually run the workflow.</p>

<div class="agent-conversation">
  <div class="agent-turn">
    <div class="agent-label">◈ prompt</div>
    <div class="agent-prompt">Find newsletters I haven't opened in three months. Show me the list, unsubscribe from the ones I confirm.</div>
    <div class="agent-runs">
      <code>mxr subscriptions --rank --format json</code>
      <code>mxr stale --within-days 90 --format ids</code>
    </div>
    <div class="agent-result">found 14 candidates ◈ you confirmed 11 ◈<span class="agent-ok">11 unsubscribed, 3 archived.</span></div>
  </div>

  <div class="agent-turn">
    <div class="agent-label">◈ prompt</div>
    <div class="agent-prompt">I'm prepping for my 1-on-1 with Sarah. Pull all email threads between us from the last two weeks, summarise the open items, draft an agenda.</div>
    <div class="agent-runs">
      <code>mxr search "from:sarah OR to:sarah after:2026-04-23" --format json</code>
      <code>mxr draft-assist &lt;thread-id&gt; "1:1 agenda"</code>
    </div>
    <div class="agent-result">7 threads, 4 with open asks ◈<span class="agent-ok">draft body generated.</span></div>
  </div>

  <div class="agent-turn">
    <div class="agent-label">◈ prompt</div>
    <div class="agent-prompt">Find every CI failure email from last week. Cross-reference with my recent commits and archive the ones whose tests have since been fixed.</div>
    <div class="agent-runs">
      <code>mxr search "from:ci@github.com after:2026-04-30" --format ids</code>
      <code>git log --since=1.week --pretty=%H %s</code>
      <code>echo &lt;ids&gt; | xargs mxr archive --yes</code>
    </div>
    <div class="agent-result">23 failures, 18 since fixed ◈<span class="agent-ok">18 archived, 5 left for review.</span></div>
  </div>
</div>

<p>This works because mxr isn't a GUI with an API bolted on. The CLI <strong>is</strong> the interface. Most reads return <code>--format json</code>, mutations support <code>--dry-run</code> and accept piped IDs, and every command composes with the rest of your shell. Your agent doesn't need browser automation, screen scraping, or a provider-specific SDK.</p>

## not a lock-in

<p>mxr is a client, not a replacement. It keeps a live two-way sync with your provider so changes flow both directions within seconds.</p>

<div class="duplex-grid">
  <div class="duplex-side">
    <div class="duplex-side-label">◈ in your other client</div>
    <ul class="duplex-actions">
      <li><span class="duplex-arrow">→</span> read a thread in Gmail web on your phone</li>
      <li><span class="duplex-arrow">→</span> apply a label in Apple Mail</li>
      <li><span class="duplex-arrow">→</span> archive a newsletter from your watch</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="duplex-bridge">
    <div class="duplex-mech">Gmail History API</div>
    <div class="duplex-mech-line">↕</div>
    <div class="duplex-mech">IMAP CONDSTORE<br/>+ IDLE</div>
  </div>
  <div class="duplex-side">
    <div class="duplex-side-label">◈ in mxr (within seconds)</div>
    <ul class="duplex-actions">
      <li><span class="duplex-arrow">←</span> mxr marks the thread read</li>
      <li><span class="duplex-arrow">←</span> the label appears in mxr's sidebar</li>
      <li><span class="duplex-arrow">←</span> the newsletter disappears from inbox</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

<p>You're not locked in either direction. Stop using mxr tomorrow and your mail is still in Gmail or your IMAP server, exactly where it was. The local archive on disk is yours to keep.</p>

## principles

<div class="principle">
  <div class="principle-text">
    <h3>One daemon, many surfaces.</h3>
    <p>The TUI, CLI, and HTTP bridge are clients of the same process. Sync, indexing, rules, snoozing, and analytics self-healing run in the background. Close the TUI; nothing stops.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="principle-margin">
    <pre><code>{`mxr             # TUI (auto-starts daemon)
mxr search ...  # CLI
mxr web         # HTTP bridge`}</code></pre>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="principle principle-flip">
  <div class="principle-text">
    <h3>A real search engine, not a filter.</h3>
    <p>Tantivy gives you sub-second BM25-ranked full-text search across your entire mailbox, with field boosts on subject, body, sender, and attachment filenames. Saved searches sit in the sidebar as programmable lenses. Optional local hybrid retrieval when you opt in.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="principle-margin">
    <pre><code>{`mxr search "subject:invoice from:vendor"
mxr search "has:attachment larger:5M"
mxr search "is:unread label:client/acme"`}</code></pre>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="principle">
  <div class="principle-text">
    <h3>Compose where you already write.</h3>
    <p>$EDITOR opens with markdown and YAML frontmatter. Your keybindings, your plugins, your muscle memory. mxr handles parsing, markdown to multipart conversion, and sending. No second editor, no janky web textarea.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="principle-margin">
    <pre><code>{`---
to:      team@example.com
subject: ship plan
---

Hi team,

We're cutting v0.5 on Friday.`}</code></pre>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="principle principle-flip">
  <div class="principle-text">
    <h3>Provider-agnostic by construction.</h3>
    <p>Gmail, IMAP, SMTP all normalise into one internal model. Mix providers freely; mxr handles the per-provider quirks behind a clean Rust trait. A conformance suite validates new adapters before they ship.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="principle-margin">
    <pre><code>{`mxr accounts add gmail
mxr accounts add imap   # work
mxr accounts add gmail  # personal
# one mailbox, one search`}</code></pre>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="principle">
  <div class="principle-text">
    <h3>Distraction-free reader mode.</h3>
    <p>Reader mode strips tracking pixels, banners, signatures, quoted text. You see what the email says, not what it is selling. One key to unsubscribe; one key to mark read and archive.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="principle-margin">
    <pre><code>{`D     unsubscribe
e     archive
m     mark read + archive
R     toggle reader mode
L     open links picker`}</code></pre>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="principle principle-flip">
  <div class="principle-text">
    <h3>Pipeable by default.</h3>
    <p>Reads support <code>--format json|jsonl|ids|csv|table</code>. Mutations support <code>--dry-run</code>, <code>--search</code> for batch ops, and accept piped IDs from stdin. See the <a href="/guides/automation-contract/">automation contract</a> for per-command capabilities.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="principle-margin">
    <pre><code>{`mxr search "from:noreply" --format ids \\
  | xargs -n1 mxr archive

mxr stale --mine --format json \\
  | jq '.[] | select(.days_stale > 30)'`}</code></pre>
  </div>
</div>

</div>

## surfaces

<p>mxr is a daemon plus clients. Three ship today; the daemon speaks JSON over a Unix socket and HTTP, so anything that can open a socket can drive your inbox.</p>

<div class="surface-stack">
  <div class="surface-block">
    <div class="surface-meta">
      <div class="surface-key">TUI</div>
      <div class="surface-tag">◈ default</div>
    </div>
    <div class="surface-body">
      <p>Vim-native keys, Gmail-style chords. Six top-level screens including a full Analytics view (storage, contacts, stale, response-time, subscriptions, wrapped). Compose in $EDITOR.</p>
      <pre class="surface-snippet"><code>mxr</code></pre>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="surface-block">
    <div class="surface-meta">
      <div class="surface-key">CLI</div>
      <div class="surface-tag">◈ scriptable</div>
    </div>
    <div class="surface-body">
      <p>Every TUI action has a CLI equivalent. Pipe through jq, xargs, anything Unix. Use it from a shell, a Makefile, your prompt, your editor, your agent.</p>
      <pre class="surface-snippet"><code>{`mxr archive --search "from:noreply older:30d" --dry-run
would archive 142 messages.`}</code></pre>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="surface-block">
    <div class="surface-meta">
      <div class="surface-key">HTTP</div>
      <div class="surface-tag">◈ browser-friendly</div>
    </div>
    <div class="surface-body">
      <p><code>mxr web</code> exposes the daemon over HTTP and WebSocket. Bearer-token auth, OpenAPI-described, generates typed SDK clients. Use the browser app we ship, or build your own client against the same API.</p>
      <pre class="surface-snippet"><code>{`curl -H "Authorization: Bearer $TOKEN" \\
    "http://127.0.0.1:7777/api/v1/mail/search?q=is:unread"
{"results": [...]}`}</code></pre>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="surface-block surface-block-fourth">
    <div class="surface-meta">
      <div class="surface-key">your client</div>
      <div class="surface-tag">◈ build it</div>
    </div>
    <div class="surface-body">
      <p>A Raycast extension. A phone app. A Slack bot. A custom dashboard. The OpenAPI spec is the contract; your agent can write the rest.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
